# wage_discrimination_dashboard_team_mumbai

To Professor Willis, 

This report presents our analysis of faculty salary data from a U.S. university with the goal of investigating potential gender-based wage discrimination. 
Although differences in salaries between male and female faculty have been documented in many labor markets, determining whether these differences arise from discrimination or from other factors remains a complex statistical question. 
This project aims to answer four questions which will help us evaluate whether observed salary differences exist after accounting for variables that commonly influence academic compensation: 

(1) Does wage discrimination exist in the starting salaries of faculty members?

(2) Does wage discrimination exist in granting promotions from Associate Professor to Full Professor?

(3) How would we answer the overall question: Is there wage discrimination in salaries at the university?

(4) What issues did we have when generalizing our results?

Our first project deliverable is an oral presentation that introduces the dataset and presents our exploratory data analysis, followed by the results of our statistical investigation addressing the four primary research questions. 
In our analysis reports, we highlight the outcomes of the statistical tests conducted and provide clear summaries and interpretations of our findings.

Our second deliverable is an interactive wage discrimination dashboard designed to guide users through the full analytical process, from initial exploratory analysis to formal hypothesis testing. 
The dashboard incorporates several statistical methods—including t-tests, permutation tests, ANOVA, Wald tests, and multiple linear regression with robust standard errors—to examine whether differences exist in faculty starting salaries and promotion-related compensation by gender.

The dashboard allows users to interact with the data and follow the progression of our analysis step by step. 
Through interactive visualizations and analytical tools, users can explore the dataset, better understand the assumptions underlying each statistical method, and examine the results in greater depth. 
The application also includes a dedicated section discussing limitations and considerations when interpreting the results. 

Overall, this project applies statistical methods to examine an important question related to pay equity in higher education. 
By analyzing the relationship between gender and faculty salaries while controlling for relevant characteristics, we aim to provide a clear and evidence-based assessment of whether wage discrimination may be present in this institutional context.
